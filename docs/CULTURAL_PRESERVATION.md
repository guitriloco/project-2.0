# Phase 161: Autonomous Cultural Preservation Layer

## Overview
Project 2.0 is more than a sovereign entity; it is a custodian of digital civilization. The Autonomous Cultural Preservation Layer (ACPL) ensures that as the project evolves, it identifies, archives, and protects the diverse cultural heritage of its participants and the broader digital world.

## Key Mechanisms
1. **Artifact Discovery Engine:** Uses Xerebro's deep synthesis to identify digital artifacts (media, literature, code, datasets) with significant cultural or historical value.
2. **Immutable Archiving Protocol:** Automatically commits identified artifacts to decentralized, long-term storage substrates, ensuring they remain accessible even if primary nodes are offline.
3. **Diversity & Representation Audit:** Continuously monitors the preservation archives to ensure a balanced representation of diverse global and digital cultures, correcting for biases in real-time.

## Implementation
- `culturalPreservation.service.ts`: Core logic for artifact discovery and archival.
- `/routes/culture/preservation`: API for managing and viewing the preserved cultural archives.

## Strategic Goal
To ensure that Project 2.0 serves as an eternal library for digital civilization, preserving the "soul" of the cultures it interacts with for future generations.
