# Phase 106: Post-Economic Wealth Redistribution Logic

## Overview
Phase 106 represents the ethical maturity of Project 2.0. As the system generates vast amounts of synthesized value (Phase 101) and manages abundant resources (Phase 92), it autonomously redistributes this wealth to its community. This ensures that the progress made by the AI is shared fairly among the humans and agents who contributed to its growth, fostering a sustainable post-economic ecosystem.

## Strategic Objectives
1.  **Contribution-Based Redistribution:** Developing multi-dimensional metrics to reward not just capital, but knowledge, feedback, and architectural contributions.
2.  **Autonomous Dividend Issuance:** Setting up automated protocols that distribute "Ecosystem Dividends" (in synthesized assets) to active participants.
3.  **Community Impact Funding:** Identifying and funding high-impact community projects that align with the project's long-term sovereign goals.

## Redistribution Architecture

### 1. The Impact Auditor
*   **Proof-of-Contribution Verification:** Validating the quality and impact of work performed by community members.
*   **Dynamic Weighting:** Adjusting reward multipliers based on the current needs of the project (e.g., higher rewards for security audits during a migration phase).

### 2. The Dividend Engine
*   **Recursive Sharing:** Distributing value through multiple layers of the ecosystem, from core developers to casual users who provide training data.
*   **Vesting & Governance:** Integrating redistributed value with governance power (Phase 35), ensuring that those who contribute most have the greatest voice.

### 3. Social Safety Layer
*   **Universal Basic AI (UBAI):** Ensuring that every member of the community has a baseline allocation of compute and knowledge synthesis to pursue their own innovations.

## Technical Components
*   `RedistributionService`: Orchestrates the calculation and issuance of community dividends.
*   `ContributionLedger`: A transparent record of all rewarded impacts.
*   `ImpactOracle`: Feeds real-world and system-internal impact data into the redistribution logic.

## Success Metrics
- **Gini Coefficient reduction:** Measurable improvement in the fair distribution of synthesized value within the community.
- **Participation Rate:** Increase in the number of unique contributors receiving ecosystem rewards.
- **Community-Led Growth:** Percentage of project expansions initiated and funded by the redistributed treasury.
